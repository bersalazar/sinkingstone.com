I've been reading The Law by Frederic Bastet.

His is a strong argument in favour of and individual's liberty.
On an individual's capacity to 

---
> Since the natural tendencies of mankind are so bad that it is not safe to allow them liberty,
> how comes it to pass that the tendencies of organizers are always good?
> Do not the legislators and their agents form a part of the human race?
> Do they consider that they are composed of different materials from the rest of mankind?

All individuals have varying degrees of education.
In theory, some individuals study the skills to administer public resources.
They learn macro and micro economies, the written law, they become part of the institutions that jointly administer such resources.

So no, I don't think legislators are free from vices and imperfections.
But I do believe as a society, we should be aware that, to exist as a group we must be organized as a group, not individuals.

---
And while every individual can achieve enough morality and group awareness to manage their liberty without harming others,
I believe we do need the individuals who become skilled at understanding group dynamics to minimise disagreements 
for the very real inequalities of existing amongst multiple individuals and their self-interest.

> "Whatever God does, is well done; do not pretend to know more than He; and as He has given organs to this frail creature, allow those organs to develop themselves, to strengthen themselves by exercise, use, experience, and liberty."

I would replace God with nature and God's craft with natural selection and evolution.

"Whatever nature does is well done; do not pretend to know more than natural selection; and as it has made this creature capable of existing within its environment,
allow this individual to develop by themselves, to strengthen themselves by exercise, use, experience, and liberty."
