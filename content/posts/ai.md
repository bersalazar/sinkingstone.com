# Cost of living is what scares musicians, not AI itself.

An artist makes art regardless of the tools they use.
Composing elements into a final product, whether a song, if done by a human, is art.

Perhaps AI-assisted is the best middle ground.

A listener, a meaningful listener, will enjoy the human art whether the artist is a millionaire or they live under a bridge.

An artist can still produce their art without AI-assistance.
They don't even need listeners, because art, for much that it helps to have spectators, exists for the sake of itself.
For the sake of the expression of a human being.

# Soundtracks for feelings

You can consider some pieces of music as "soundtracks for feelings", where those feelings are expressed in the written form, and then by a vocal performance.

The music becomes complementary. A vehicle.
The power of the piece of art comes from the words.

Some vehicles are far better than others.
They can enhance the feelings expressed in the words, and can even take personality themselves.

It's better to have examples.
Take Bob Dylan, a music composer that even was awarded the Nobel prize for Literature.
He's that good at meaningful words.

His music however, in many cases, is a vehicle.
A soundtrack for his deeply philosophical and impactful stories and reflections.
If you listen to "The Times They Are A-Changing", "Mr. Tambourine Man" and "Blowin' In The Wind" without words, it's hard to differentiate which one is which.
They're formulaic means for extremely important words.

That's not say there's no beauty in his compositions or arrangements.
There's **many** Bob Dylan songs that have beautiful arrangements. e.g. "Most Of The Time"

This is, of course, taking into account original recordings and the artist's original intention.
It doesn't consider covers, which may introduce more intentional and protagonistic musical arrangements to complement the words.

It's a careful dance.
But the point is we don't listen Bob Dylan, Joni Mitchell  for his catchy tunes.
We listen because he's got something very meaningful to say.

Here's where solo, ballad-style, artists thrive.

Like in a movie, or audiovisual media, the "audio" part is generally not a protagonist.
Audio is an element, or even considered an enhancer for what we are watching, the visual part.
The feelings.

My mother-in-law, who doesn't understand much english, doesn't like songs that are in english.
It's because she's connecting with the words. She's engaging with musicalized feelings in written form, enhanced by melody, harmonies and textures from various instruments.

She's not connecting with the music without the lyrics, she's connecting with lyrics that happen to navigate in a sea of music.

Some songs achieve balancing spectacular music composition and arrangements with immensely meaningful words.
An incredible talent, in my opinion.

Some don't.
Some music is an ice-skating ring, inert.
The words are a skier, being beautiful and meaningful.

# Music as soundtrack for life

This ties itself to cost of living for musicians.

Music that stirs emotions, either by the music itself or by the act where it's used (e.g. live concerts), generates money.

## Merengue Los Ilegales

Songs produced by bands like Ilegales, at least their merengue styles, are meant for dancing.

You can notice it in the structure of some of their hit songs.

The song's sections are extended to allow for people to stand up, grab a partner and dance for a while.

This is one of those acts.
