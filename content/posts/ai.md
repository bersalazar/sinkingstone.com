# Cost of living is what scares musicians, not AI itself.

An artist makes art.
Regardless of the tools they use.
Composing elements into a final piece of art, a song, if done by a human, is art.

Perhaps AI-assisted is the best middle ground.

A listener, a meaningful listener, will enjoy the human art whether the artist is a millionaire or they live under a bridge.

An artist can still produce their art without AI-assistance.
They don't even need listeners, because art, for much that it helps to have spectators, exists for the sake of itself.
And for the sake of the expression of a human being.

# Soundtracks for feelings

You can consider some pieces of music as "soundtracks for feelings".

Where those feelings are expressed by the written word and then by the vocal performance.

The music is complementary, but the power comes from the words.

Here's where solo, ballad-style, artists thrive.

Here's where my mother-in-law, who doesn't speak english, doesn't like music that's in english.

It's because she's connecting with the words, that happen to be musicalized.

She's not connecting with the music without the lyrics, she's connecting with lyrics that happen to navigate in a sea of music.

Some songs achieve the incredibly talented way of balancing spectacular music composition and arrangements with immensely meaningful words., 

Some don't, some music is an ice-skating ring, inert.
The skier is the lyrics, being beautiful and talented.

# Music as soundtrack for life

This ties itself to cost of living for musicians.

Music that stirs emotions, either by the music itself or by the act where it's used (e.g. live concerts), generates money.

## Merengue Los Ilegales

Songs produced by bands like Ilegales, at least their merengue styles, are meant for dancing.

You can notice it in the structure of some of their hit songs.

The song's sections are extended to allow for people to stand up, grab a partner and dance for a while.

This is one of those acts.
